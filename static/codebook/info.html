6.1 Quality of Communication with Healthcare Providers

Respondents were asked the following question, “The following questions are about your communication with all doctors, nurses, or other health professionals you saw during the past 12 months. How often did they do each of the following?” with the following possible responses:

  • Never
  • Sometimes
  • Usually
  • Always

(#fig:comm_quality-fig)Distribution of responses regarding the quality of communication with healthcare providers

A simple index was created for the quality of communication with health care providers. A simple analysis of the correlation among responses to these five questions found that all pairwise correlations were greater than 0.7, suggesting there was little variation in the responses to these questions. Because of this, it may make sense to use an index during analyses rather than the individual questions.

6.2 Health Care Information

6.2.1 Trust of Information

Respondents were asked the following question, “In general, how much would you trust information about health or medical topics from each of the following?” with the following possible responses:

  • Not at all
  • A little
  • Some
  • A lot

The following sources of questions were specifically asked about:

  • A doctor
  • Family or friends
  • The internet
  • Magazines, newspapers, or television
  • Government health agencies
  • Religious organizations and leaders

The correlation suggests that there is some clustering of answers to questions. Specifically, the trust of the information a doctor provides is not strongly correlated with any other questions. Trust of the information provided through the internet, media, and government are well correlated with one another. Trust of information provided by friends and family members along with information from religious organizations are somewhat correlated with other questions.

6.2.2 Confidence in Getting Information

Respondents were asked the following question, “Overall, how confident are you that you could get advice or information about health or medical topics if you needed it?” with the following possible responses:

  • Not confident at all
  • A little confident
  • Somewaht confident
  • Very confident
  • Completely confident

(#fig:info_confidence-fig)Responses to confidence in getting information question

6.2.3 Sources of Information

Respondents were asked the following question, “What source(s) do you use when you are looking for information about health or medical topics (please check all that apply)?” and given the option to either reply “Yes” or “No.”

They were asked about the following sources of information:

  • Books
  • Brochures, pamphlets, etc.
  • Family
  • Friend/Co-worker
  • Doctor or healthcare provider
  • Internet search (Google, Wikipedia, etc.)
  • Library
  • Magazines
  • Newspapers or news websites
  • Peer-reviewed scientific journals
  • I do not look for information about health or medical topics

The following figure summarizes the proportion of respondents indicating “Yes” for each of the sources.

(#fig:info-sources_ord-fig)Distribution of responses the sources of information questions

6.2.4 Searching for Information

Respondents were asked the following question, “When you search for information about health or medical topics, how much do you agree or disagree with each of the following statements?” and given the following response options:

  • Strongly agree
  • Agree
  • Neither agree nor disagree
  • Disagree
  • Strongly disagree

Respondents were specifically asked about these three statements:

  • It takes a lot of effort to get the information needed
  • I feel frustrated during my search for information
  • The information found is hard to understand
