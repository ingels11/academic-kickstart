2.1 Age

Age was entered in an open-ended field and respondents were screened into the survey as 40 years of age or older. The variable demo_age_cont comes directly from this entry by each respondent. Additionally, a binary variable was created indicating age 65 or older (demo_age_65_bin) and an ordinal variable classifying age into ten categories by 5-year intervals to match Census reports (demo_age_ord).

New Variable Brief Description
demo_age_cont Age of respondent as continuous variable
demo_age_ord Age of respondnet as ordinal variable
demo_age_65_bin Age of 65 years or greater

The continuous and ordinal variables created for age are summarized in the following two figures. The histogram shows age in one year chunks and reveals that the number of individuals in a given age does not begin to consistently decrease until around age 65. After age 65, the number of individuals begins to tail off creating a right-skew in the distribution of age. For the binary variable, 652 (26.0%) respodents were 65 years of age or older.

2.3 Type of health insurance

Survey respondents indicated type of health insurance via a checkbox style selection where multiple responses could be made, including an “Other” option where they would be prompted with a textbox entry. The possible repsonses were as follows (with the shorthand that will be used to refer to the category in parentheses):

  • Insurance through a current or former employee (Employer)
  • Insurance purchased directly from an insurance company (Self)
  • Insurance purchased through a federal or state exchange (Exchange)
  • Tricare, other military healthcare, or VA (Military)
  • Medicaid (Medicaid)
  • Medicare (Medicare)
  • Other (please specify) (Other)
  • I don’t know (Unknown)

The goal in processing this variable was to take the text entries and classify as many as possible into one of the responses provided in the survey. Additionally, some text responses indicated participation in a supplemental Medicare program and other responses into categories of insurance not included in the survey options. A final set of binary variables was then created for each of the first 6 survey responses where a 1 is assigned if the participant indicated that type of insurance or if the text response indicated that type of insurance.

To classify the text entries, a set of text strings were used to search respondent entries and when one of these strings was found in the text entry, classify that respondent with one of the 6 types of health insurance. The following table summarizes this classification process.

The presence of two text categories, “Private” and “Public” show a possible connection with multiple survey options. In this case, these responses can not be clearly tied to one of the 6 options on the survey but can clearly be tied to a category when these responses are collapsed in other variable creation.

A set of binary variables was created, corresponding to each of the possible survey responses. Each respondent could indicate one or more of these survey responses and as a result, each respondent may have a value of 1 in one or more of the binary variables. Furthermore, if the text response for an individual indicates one of these survey responses would have been appropriate, then the respondent was also given a value of 1 for that variable. For text entries that could only be classified as Private or Public and not one of the other text categories, no classifications to one of the following binary variables was made. One binary variable was created that does not have a directly associated survey response for those individuals responding as having Medicare and indicating supplemental insurance in their text entry.

Examples:

  • Respondent provides survey responses of Employer and Medicare then this individual would have values of 1 for demo_ins_employer_bin and demo_ins_medicare_bin
  • Respondent provides a survey response of Other and a text entry of “Purchased through covered california” then this individual would have values of 1 for demo_ins_exchange_bin
  • Note: this text entry would be tagged as Private and Exchange (see table above)
  • Respondent provides a survey response of Other and a text entry of “Humana” then this individual would have a value of 1 for demo_ins_other_bin
  • Note: this text entry would be tagged as Public (see table above)
